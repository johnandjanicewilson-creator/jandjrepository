It was finally time. After the move and the long business of getting settled in our new home, we were ready to point the car north and go, a road trip to see family and friends with a few special adventures along the way.

We got an early start for the five-hour drive to Williamsburg, where Janice's cousin Kathy and her husband Eddie Luzzi live. Just as in the years we had the RV, Janice wanted the backroads that run through the backbone of the local communities instead of sliding past them. Our map app had some crazy ideas about how to manage it. It would send us off a perfectly good road to make a right, then two blocks later a left, and it went on that way until we were laughing at ourselves winding down one side street after another. The funny part was that no matter how many turns it invented, the arrival time on the screen never changed a bit.

We pulled in around one o'clock and sat a good while, catching up on both families' news. Eddie, it turned out, is a volunteer at a place called Dream Catchers, "Where Horse Power Changes Lives," and they suggested we take a ride out to see it.

**Dream Catchers.**

![Eddie and Kathy Luzzi at the Dream Catchers welcome sign](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/dreamcatchers-eddie-kathy-sign.png)

It is a short drive from Williamsburg out to Toano, Virginia. The mission there is to help the people who need it most, folks with special needs, at-risk young people, and veterans, through therapy and learning carried out alongside the horses. The place is beautiful, the barns clean and well kept, tended mostly by volunteers, with a manager and a few experienced full-time hands. Many of the horses are on loan or have been donated to the program.

![Gameboy, a Canadian Sport Horse on loan to Dream Catchers from Mary Millhiser and sponsored by Ben Benabise](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/dreamcatchers-gameboy.jpg)

Eddie volunteers three days a week, and you could hear the pride in it as he showed us around. He is learning the horses from the ground up. He started by cooling them down after their rides, and now he tacks and grooms them; his next step will be leading the horses with the riders up top. What a wonderful thing for Eddie to give his time.

We drove back to the house for a fabulous lamb chop dinner and a few glasses of wine, just a few, ha. It was a wonderful evening with family. In the morning we would be off early again, our next stop a night's sleep in New Haven, Connecticut.
