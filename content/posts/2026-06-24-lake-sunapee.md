As we pulled away from visiting Janice's uncle, a call came in from Lee, the husband of Janice's sister, Connie. Janice had told him we were already on the road and planned to meet them for dinner around 5:30. Apparently there had been a misunderstanding. Lee explained that they now eat their dinner around 2:30, which was new news to us, so our timing did not work out. We ended up canceling and were on our own for the evening. It was a little disappointing, because Janice had been looking forward to time with Connie and Lee.

We arrived in Derry and had dinner at Sabatino's North, an Italian restaurant nearby. We were pleasantly surprised by how good it was. After sharing a bottle of wine and a relaxing meal, we headed back to the hotel.

That night we stayed near their home at a new hotel called Garner, part of the IHG family. It was another pleasant surprise, new, clean, and exceptionally comfortable, giving us one of the best nights of sleep we had on the whole trip.

Later that evening, Janice called Connie to confirm the next morning's plan. We were all set to pick her up at 9:00 for the drive to Lake Sunapee.

Breakfast the next morning was the usual hot buffet, but the eggs, the meat, and the rest were noticeably better than what we usually find at hotels like this.

After breakfast we packed up and headed up the road to pick up Connie for the drive from Derry to Lake Sunapee; Lee was not up for the trip this time. One of the main reasons for the visit was to see Janice's mother's new headstone and to clean her father's. It also gave us a chance to spend time in a place that had been Janice's parents' home for more than thirty years.

When her mother first passed away, the headstone had been engraved with only her maiden name and an incorrect date of death. Janice knew her mother would have wanted it set right. The headstone had been provided by the government, since both she and Janice's father were World War II veterans. The Department of Veterans Affairs, which furnishes veterans' headstones, would not replace it, so Janice found a monument company near Sunapee that made an identical stone with the correct information. Seeing it finally in place brought a real sense of peace and closure.

![Two military headstones set side by side, cleaned and cared for during a memorial visit](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/roberts-gravestones-jeanne-stanley.jpg)

We cleaned the headstones and took a few photographs together at the family bench.

![Janice and her sister Connie at the Roberts family bench in the Sunapee cemetery](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/sun-Connie_and_janice.jpg)

![Connie beside the Roberts bench, with flags and peonies at the family plot](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/sun-connie_cemetery.jpg)

Then we headed to the Sunapee Harbor docks for lunch. The restaurant is under new management and has been nicely renovated, cleaner and more modern, though it has lost some of the quirky charm Janice remembered from visits over the past forty years. The food was good, but for Janice, that old atmosphere was part of what made the place special.

Of course, no trip to Sunapee would be complete without the little shops along the harbor. We stopped at the store that sells Lake Sunapee shirts, gifts, and souvenirs, where John found a lightweight long-sleeve shirt he liked. We also made our traditional stop at the candy store. It offers far more than candy these days, but it still carries plenty of old-fashioned treats along with fun gifts and trinkets.

![The docks and shops along Sunapee Harbor](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/sun-harbor.jpg)

By mid-afternoon it was time to take Connie home and say our goodbyes. Janice was so glad for the time with her; she feels Connie is her best friend, and those moments are precious. From there we turned south and continued toward home, with our next overnight stop in Mystic, Connecticut.

As the years pass, getting all of Janice's siblings together grows harder. We hope that next year everyone can gather again for another day of sharing old memories and making new ones.
