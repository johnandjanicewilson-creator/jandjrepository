After leaving Williamsburg, we drove to New Haven, Connecticut, and spent the night. After many hours on the road, we decided that was enough driving for one day.

The next morning we headed for Cape Cod to meet Janice's brother, Brian, and his girlfriend, Andrea, who had spent the weekend exploring Boston. Brian had reserved rooms for us at the Ocean Mist Hotel in Yarmouth, right on the ocean, so we could spend a little time together before continuing on our separate ways.

Since we arrived a few hours before they did, we went looking for lunch and stumbled across Mac Daddy's, a colorful little diner that looked like it had been transported straight from the 1950s.

![Mac Daddy's Diner, a red 1950s-style diner on Cape Cod](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-mac_daddy_outside.jpg)

The diner is well known for its Eggs Benedict, and the Irish Benedict had outstanding reviews. When our meals arrived, we looked at each other and agreed at once that we should have shared one. It was a huge serving, piled high with homemade corned beef hash that had very few potatoes and lots of flavorful corned beef. It was excellent. Inside, the diner was as entertaining as the food. Vintage decorations covered the walls, and little tabletop speakers played Elvis and other old favorites, making it feel like we had stepped back in time.

We planned to spend the afternoon relaxing on the beach with Brian and Andrea, but Mother Nature had other ideas. Rain kept us indoors, so instead we enjoyed the beautiful ocean view from our balconies, shared a few glasses of wine, caught up on everyone's recent adventures, and even squeezed in a short afternoon nap.

![The beach and ocean seen from our room at the Ocean Mist in Yarmouth](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-view-from-room.jpg)


That evening we headed to Skipper's, a seafood restaurant that had come highly recommended. Janice was convinced we needed to drive out to the main road, even though the GPS insisted otherwise. After wandering around for several minutes, we discovered the restaurant was practically next door to our hotel. Sometimes it is best to just trust the GPS.

Our little detour turned out to be a blessing. When we arrived, the wait for a table was about seventy-five minutes, so we headed upstairs to the bar. Just then, the staff began clearing a table for six. Two ladies were waiting for it, so we asked how many were in their party.

"Just the two of us," they replied.

"There are four of us," we said.

Without hesitation, they smiled and invited us to join them.

What could have been a long wait turned into one of the most enjoyable dinners of the trip. We spent the evening laughing, swapping travel stories, and getting to know two strangers who, for one evening, felt like old friends.

![Brian and Andrea with the two ladies who shared their table at Skipper's](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-ladies_at_skippers_with_B_and_andrea.jpg)

It is funny how travel works sometimes. Had we arrived a few minutes earlier, or a few minutes later, we probably never would have met them.

Dinner was excellent. Brian knew it was not really steamer season but ordered them anyway. They were much larger than the sweet little steamers New England is famous for, and not quite what he had expected. Andrea ordered a lobster roll, which, as always, was outstanding.

![John, Janice, Brian, and Andrea at the bar at Skipper's on Cape Cod](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-4-of-us-in-bar.jpg)

The next morning we woke to a cloudy but beautiful day. Before checking out, we carried our coffee and tea down to the beach and took a peaceful walk along the shoreline. The beach was quiet, the waves rolled gently onto the sand, and even without sunshine, Cape Cod has a beauty all its own.

![Andrea and Brian walking the shoreline on a cloudy Cape Cod morning](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-andrea-brian-walking-beach.jpg)

From there we drove a few miles to visit Janice's uncle, Bill Otis. At ninety-eight years old, he still lives independently in a senior community and looks terrific. We had planned to take him out for lunch, but he had been a little dizzy and was not quite up to going out. Instead, we spent the next three hours in his apartment, talking, laughing, and reminiscing about family stories from years gone by.

![Uncle Bill Otis, ninety-eight, telling stories from his rocking chair](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-uncle-bill-story-telling.jpg)

Bill's memory is still remarkable. Now and then he would pause while searching for a word, then grin and say, "My cloud is having problems." We all laughed, because honestly, most of us can relate.

One of the best moments came when Brian mentioned that Bill's son Brian and he had once shared an apartment while working on offshore oil rigs in Houston. Uncle Bill had never heard the story, and it opened the door to even more family memories.

Looking around his apartment, Bill proudly showed us his collection of cacti. Janice joked that she never knew her cousin Brian had become such a cactus farmer. Then Bill mentioned, almost in passing, that he had recently taken up painting.

He led us into another room filled with his artwork, and we were genuinely impressed.

![Uncle Bill's painting corner by the window, crowded with brushes and works in progress](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-bill-painting-area.jpg)

![A seascape in progress on Uncle Bill's easel](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-bill-easel.jpg)

![Shelves of Uncle Bill's finished landscape paintings](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-bill-finished-art.jpg)

Several of his paintings were beautiful, especially the one with bright yellow flowers that caught our eye right away. It was inspiring to see someone discover a new hobby and become so accomplished at ninety-eight. Room after room filled with paintings was the last thing we expected.

![Andrea and Brian with Uncle Bill in his apartment](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-andrea-bill-brian.jpg)

All too soon it was time to say goodbye. Three hours had passed in what felt like a few minutes. Brian was heading north to meet a friend in Bath, Maine, while we continued on to New Hampshire, where Janice's sister Connie and her husband Lee were waiting to have dinner with us.

Before leaving, we gathered for one last family picture.

![John and Janice with Uncle Bill, one last family picture before goodbye](https://res.cloudinary.com/dsru5pryd/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/images/2026/cape-john-bill-janice.jpg)

As we drove away, we could not help but feel grateful. Cape Cod had given us wonderful food, beautiful ocean views, unexpected new friends, and, most of all, an unforgettable afternoon with family. Those are the moments that make a trip truly memorable.

Until next year, goodbye Cape Cod.
